Evaluation

Evaluation


We created a dataset with 50 training examples to evaluate the accuracy of our predictions. Among the data points there were also the 3 locations represented by the 3 red dots above. These entries were all mapped to logins made on Thursdays, at times 12:00, 12:47 and 12:23 respectively.

Our query corresponded to a login made at the location of the blue dot, on a Thursday at 12:30. Our assumption was that this should be accepted as a legitimate attempt, because its characteristics follow the pattern set by user behaviour previously recorded, represented by the 3 red dots. Our model successfully authenticated our attempt, and stored the entry as another event.

The margin with which the location accuracy is being calculated is defined by two distinct approaches. The location longitude and latitude coordinate numbers that are being stored contain 4 decimal places, which leads to an accuracy of up to 11 meters, and the location numbers in the query have 5 decimal places, which represents an accuracy of 1 meter. This leads to a slightly bigger area that will accept logins. The second criteria is the nu parameter, set in engine.json, that dictates the ratio of outliers to support vectors.
