Background and Context - IBM Insight

In the mobile and internet banking market today, there are as many uncertainties as opportunities - IT and business leaders still think that they need to choose between:

  • developing as many apps as possible as quickly as they can VS ensuring a good quality
  • robust apps VS being a fast innovator
  • great user interface VS solid backend

As the market is evolving, banks now have other competitors, not just other banks, such as Google, Apple and Facebook. Even in areas where the market has not taken off yet, like mobile payments, the question is “when”, not “if”.

Replacing credit cards with a phone is not that interesting in itself. However, by adding intelligence to digital wallets i.e. contextual awareness, location-based offers, mobile banking solution providers will create a more compelling and personalised customer experience

Almost all of the banks in the world have mobile and online banking services. With the introduction of the Apple Watch and new banking apps, mobile banking has never been easier for the customer. Digitisation of the banking industry is big business today and with fierce competition across the finance sector, there is a demand for new and innovative solutions.
